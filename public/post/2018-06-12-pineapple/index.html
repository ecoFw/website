Sugar Cane

  • Sugar was the driving force in colonization of the New World, the dominance of Great Britain in the West Indies
  • In the South and the North, sugar was a highly lucrative industry that drove slave trade
  • Sugar cane was the primary food that drove land conversion and industry in Hawaii
  • A group of American entrepreneurs and missionaries who were largely built on sugar interests eventually orchestrated significant blows to the Hawaii monarchy
  • This included the Reciprocity Treaty, which opened Hawaiian sugar markets to the US, and the Bayonett Treaty, including the abdication of Queen Lili’o’kalani
  • Queen Lili’o’kalani contested her abdication and with support from the Clevland Administration, a former confederate, southern democratic senator, James H. Blount, was dispatched to Hawaii
  • Over 100 years later, the Blount Report was the basis of some of the early apologies to first people
